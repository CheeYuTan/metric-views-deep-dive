## Closing Thoughts

The main lesson from this first post is simple:

Trusted AI/BI does not start at query time.

It starts earlier, when an organization makes it clear where trusted context lives.

Discover and Domains are a practical first layer because they organize assets around business responsibility instead of technical hierarchy. For a risk and compliance team, that means users can start from Credit Risk, Fraud Risk, AML and KYC, Operational Risk, or Regulatory Reporting instead of guessing which catalog or schema contains the right asset.

The most important part is not the domain card itself. It is the combination of signals around it:

- Business descriptions
- Subdomains
- Curated sections
- Certified assets
- Deprecated assets
- Owners
- Metric Views
- Dashboards
- Genie Spaces
- Apps and notebooks

Together, these signals help both humans and AI/BI experiences start from better context.

In the next post, I will go one layer deeper: after a user finds the right domain, how do we define certified KPIs that every dashboard, analyst, and agent can reuse?

That is where Metric Views become the core semantic layer.
